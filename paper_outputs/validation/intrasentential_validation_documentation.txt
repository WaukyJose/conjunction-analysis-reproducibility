INTRA-SENTENTIAL INDEX VALIDATION STEP

Purpose
This step validated whether the intra-sentential indices were measuring the intended construct: within-sentence conjunctive relations, especially clause-linking relations associated with paratactic and hypotactic taxis.

Validation design
Two manually checked samples were used:

1. Random text cases
- 300 cases total.
- 100 cases from COREFL.
- 100 cases from EFCAMDAT.
- 100 cases from GiG.
- Purpose: estimate general validity of the intra-sentential parser across corpora.

2. Risky item cases
- 150 cases total.
- Oversampled multifunctional/problematic connectors: and, as, but, or, since, so, then, when, while.
- Purpose: test the most error-prone lexical items.

Main findings
Random text cases:
- 300/300 rows annotated.
- 69.0% of broad detections were valid.
- Supported cases improved validity to 80.0%.
- Supported cases improved clause-linking precision to 76.4% when clear and partially clause-like cases were combined.

Risky item cases:
- 150/150 rows annotated.
- 62.7% of all risky detections were valid.
- Supported risky cases showed very high validity: 96.9%.
- Unsupported/broad risky cases showed much lower validity: 36.5%.

Interpretation
The validation confirms a clear distinction between the broad detection layer and the supported/primed layer.

Broad layer:
- Useful as exploratory evidence of general within-sentence connector presence.
- Includes phrase-level coordination and other non-clause-linking cases.
- Should be reported cautiously.

Supported layer:
- Much more reliable for clause-linking-oriented interpretation.
- Should be used as the main inferential/statistical layer for intra-sentential analyses.

Main false-positive types
- and/or linking noun phrases, adjectives, names, or list items.
- so used as an intensifier, e.g., “so cute”, “so much”, “so sorry”.
- as in expressions such as “such as”, “as well”, “working as”, “considered as”.
- then used in number sequences, sentence-final adverbials, or expressions such as “by then”.
- while in noun phrases such as “a while”.
- because of and after + noun phrase, where the connector does not introduce a finite clause.
- token-boundary errors, e.g., and in “standing” or “husband”, or in “story/director/for”, as in “was/has”.

Conclusion
The intra-sentential validation supports using the supported/primed indices as the main validated indices for statistical interpretation. The broad indices may still be retained as exploratory descriptive measures, but they should not be interpreted as reliable clause-linking measures without the supported filter.
